Build a new ACP module called “Political Compass.”

Goal:
Create a quick 20-question quiz that places users on a 2-axis political compass:
1. Economic Scale: Left ↔ Right
2. Social Scale: Libertarian ↔ Authoritarian

The module should be neutral, nonpartisan, educational, and designed to help users understand their own political leanings without forcing party labels.

Core Requirements:
- Add a new page/module: /political-compass
- Use ACP’s existing design system and Liquid Glass styling.
- Show a short intro explaining the quiz.
- Ask 20 questions total.
- Each question uses a 5-point Likert scale:
  - Strongly Disagree
  - Disagree
  - Neutral / Unsure
  - Agree
  - Strongly Agree
- After completion, calculate the user’s position on a 2D compass.
- Show:
  - Economic score from -10 to +10
  - Social score from -10 to +10
  - Compass chart with a plotted dot
  - Plain-English result summary
  - Optional shareable result card

Scoring System:
Each answer should convert to a numeric value:

Strongly Disagree = -2
Disagree = -1
Neutral / Unsure = 0
Agree = +1
Strongly Agree = +2

Each question has:
- axis: "economic" or "social"
- direction: +1 or -1

Final score formula:
questionScore = answerValue * direction

Economic raw score = sum of all economic question scores
Social raw score = sum of all social question scores

Normalize each axis to a -10 to +10 scale:

normalizedScore = (rawScore / maxPossibleRawScoreForAxis) * 10

Economic interpretation:
-10 = strongly economic left
0 = mixed/centrist economics
+10 = strongly economic right

Social interpretation:
-10 = strongly libertarian/civil-liberties oriented
0 = mixed/centrist social views
+10 = strongly authoritarian/order-oriented

Compass Quadrants:
Economic Left + Libertarian:
“Community Libertarian”
Supports economic fairness, public investment, civil liberties, and decentralized power.

Economic Left + Authoritarian:
“State Progressive”
Supports economic intervention, public programs, regulation, and stronger government authority.

Economic Right + Libertarian:
“Market Libertarian”
Supports free markets, low regulation, individual freedom, and limited government power.

Economic Right + Authoritarian:
“National Conservative”
Supports market-oriented economics, traditional institutions, law/order, and stronger national authority.

Near Center:
“Pragmatic Centrist”
Mixed views across economics and social issues. May prefer issue-by-issue decision-making.

UI Flow:
1. Intro screen:
Title: “Find Your Political Compass”
Subtitle: “Answer 20 quick questions to see where your views fall on economic and social issues.”
Button: “Start Quiz”

2. Question screen:
- Show progress bar: Question X of 20
- Show question text
- Show 5 answer buttons
- Allow Back and Next
- Store answers in component state

3. Results screen:
Show:
- Compass chart
- Economic score
- Social score
- Quadrant label
- Short explanation
- Button: “Retake Quiz”
- Button: “Share Result”

Compass Chart:
- X-axis = Economic Left to Economic Right
- Y-axis = Social Authoritarian at top, Libertarian at bottom
- Plot user dot at:
  x = economicScore
  y = socialScore
- Center lines at 0
- Label quadrants:
  - Top Left: State Progressive
  - Top Right: National Conservative
  - Bottom Left: Community Libertarian
  - Bottom Right: Market Libertarian

Important:
For visual display, social authoritarian should appear higher on the chart, libertarian lower.

Data Storage:
If user is logged in:
- Save latest compass result to their profile.
- Store:
  - economicScore
  - socialScore
  - quadrant
  - completedAt
  - answer set
If user is not logged in:
- Allow quiz without account.
- Store result locally in browser only.
- Prompt user to create account to save result.

Privacy:
- Do not make results public by default.
- Add toggle:
  “Show my compass result on my profile”
Default: OFF

Admin/Developer Notes:
- Keep scoring logic in a separate utility function.
- Keep question list in a separate config file.
- Make the system easy to expand from 20 to 40 questions later.
- Add unit tests for scoring normalization.

Result Labels:
Use these thresholds:
- If absolute economic score < 2 and absolute social score < 2:
  “Pragmatic Centrist”
- If economic < -2 and social < -2:
  “Community Libertarian”
- If economic < -2 and social > 2:
  “State Progressive”
- If economic > 2 and social < -2:
  “Market Libertarian”
- If economic > 2 and social > 2:
  “National Conservative”
- Otherwise:
  “Mixed / Issue-by-Issue Voter”

Result Summary Generator:
Create a function that returns a short explanation based on score.

Example:
Economic Left:
“You tend to support stronger public investment, worker protections, and government action to reduce inequality.”

Economic Right:
“You tend to support lower regulation, private enterprise, and market-based solutions.”

Social Libertarian:
“You tend to prioritize civil liberties, personal freedom, privacy, and limits on government authority.”

Social Authoritarian:
“You tend to prioritize order, tradition, national security, and stronger institutional authority.”

Neutral:
“Your answers suggest a mixed or balanced position on this axis.”

Add disclaimer:
“This quiz is educational and simplified. Political views are complex and may not fit perfectly into any single chart.”
